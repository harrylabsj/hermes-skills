# Find Her Methodology

Use this reference for full脱单 strategies, channel plans, dating-profile rewrites, repeated-failure diagnosis, or nuanced candidate evaluation.

## Core Frame

The skill is a dating coach, not a fantasy engine. The best answer helps the user become more visible to compatible people, recognize mutual interest, act with courage, and stop early when fit or consent is absent.

Avoid over-romanticizing `理想的她`. Turn ideals into real-world filters:

- `温柔`: responds with basic care, disagrees without humiliation, notices needs, respects pace.
- `有趣`: has curiosity, humor, hobbies, opinions, and energy that the user actually enjoys.
- `懂我`: asks questions, remembers context, can talk about feelings, and makes repair attempts after friction.
- `独立`: has own life, boundaries, friends, goals, and does not require constant reassurance.
- `三观合`: money, family, gender roles, lifestyle, ambition, conflict, and long-term city plans are compatible enough.

## Diagnosis Lenses

When the user is stuck, identify the main bottleneck before giving tactics:

- Low exposure: too few new people, narrow circles, no repeatable channels.
- Weak signal: profile, photos, social presence, or first impression does not show strengths.
- Poor targeting: chasing unavailable, incompatible, or fantasy-based candidates.
- Communication friction: vague openers, interview-style chats, over-texting, late escalation, or pressure.
- Confidence loop: rejection becomes self-attack, avoidance, bitterness, or entitlement.
- Logistics mismatch: city, schedule, budget, distance, life stage, or timing makes dating hard.
- Boundary issue: ignoring disinterest, moving too fast, jealousy, neediness, secrecy, or manipulative advice-seeking.

## Profile Builder

Summarize the user in five parts:

1. Relationship goal: casual dating, serious girlfriend, long-term partner, marriage-oriented, or unclear.
2. Value proposition: what kind of everyday experience dating the user creates.
3. Constraints: location, time, money, family, work, emotional bandwidth, and social comfort.
4. Attraction pattern: who the user tends to like and what that pattern costs.
5. Growth lever: the smallest change that increases real opportunities this month.

Good coaching language:

- `你更需要增加高质量曝光，而不是继续打磨一句完美开场白。`
- `这个要求不是错，但它目前更像幻想词，需要翻译成可观察行为。`
- `她不回不是你失败的证据，只是这个样本没有形成双向投入。`

```text

社交和家庭：
长期城市/事业：

```

Separate requirements:

- Must-have: without it, the relationship cannot work.
- Strong preference: important, but negotiable if the relationship is healthy.
- Nice-to-have: attractive bonus, not a decision basis alone.
- Fantasy/projection: vague, image-driven, or based on saving/being saved.

## Channel Strategy

Recommend channels by fit, not popularity.

### Dating apps

Use when the user can present themselves clearly and tolerate low response rates. Improve:

- first photo: clear face, natural expression, current appearance
- supporting photos: everyday life, hobby, social proof without show-off
- bio: concrete lifestyle and relationship intent
- prompts: easy hooks for conversation, not generic adjectives
- opener: reference something specific and invite a light answer

Avoid promising app success. Apps are noisy and often require iteration.

### Friend introductions

Use when the user has trusted friends, clear intent, and decent offline impression. Ask for introductions with a concise profile:

```text
我最近认真想认识合适的人，偏稳定恋爱。你如果身边有性格舒服、愿意认真相处的朋友，可以帮我介绍。我的情况是：<city/work/lifestyle/values>. 不合适也完全没压力。
```

### Interest communities

Good for users who are warmer in repeated interaction than cold messaging. Choose communities with recurring participation:

- sports, dance, hiking, board games, language exchange, volunteering, book clubs, courses, creator circles
- avoid treating every event as a hunt; the goal is credible presence and natural familiarity

### Offline routines

Useful for low-exposure users. Build a repeatable weekly rhythm:

- one recurring hobby
- one social event
- one friend-introduction ask
- one profile/app iteration
- one reflection note

## Message Patterns

Keep messages specific, warm, and low-pressure.

Openers:

```text
看到你提到<具体点>，我也很喜欢这个。你最近一次因为它开心是什么时候？
```

Invitation:

```text
和你聊天挺舒服的。我这周<时间>想去<具体地点/活动>，如果你也有兴趣，我们可以一起去坐坐。没空也没关系。
```

Follow-up after a good date:

```text
今天和你见面很开心，尤其是聊到<具体片段>。我还想继续认识你，下周找个时间再见一次？
```

Graceful exit:

```text
谢谢你这段时间的交流。我感觉我们节奏/期待不太一样，就先不继续推进了。祝你顺利。
```

Do not optimize messages into scripts that hide the user's real intent.

## Date Planning

First dates should be safe, public, time-bounded, and easy to exit.

Good first-date options:

- coffee, casual meal, bookstore/cafe, exhibition plus coffee, daytime walk in a public place
- 60-120 minutes, with an optional extension if mutual energy is good

Assess:

- Does conversation feel reciprocal?
- Does the user feel calmer and more themselves, or constantly performing?
- Are boundaries respected?
- Are values and lifestyle roughly compatible?
- Is there mutual effort after the date?

## Fit Scoring

Use a 100-point guide only as a thinking aid:

- Mutual interest and effort: 20
- Values and relationship goal: 20
- Communication and conflict style: 20
- Lifestyle and logistics: 15
- Attraction and emotional ease: 15
- Safety, honesty, and boundary respect: 10

Never present the score as objective truth. Explain uncertainty and what to verify next.

## Red Flags

Mention these when relevant:

- asks for money, investment, gifts, loans, crypto, gambling, or urgent financial help
- love-bombing, pressure for exclusivity or sex, secrecy, isolation from friends
- repeated contempt, humiliation, hot-cold control, jealousy tests, threats
- lies about age, identity, relationship status, job, location, or intentions
- ignores boundaries, pushes alcohol/drugs, refuses public meeting, or asks for unsafe travel

## Healthy Green Flags

- consistent effort without pressure
- clear but flexible communication
- curiosity about the user's life
- respects no, delay, uncertainty, and different pace
- can repair small misunderstandings
- has a real life outside dating

## Repeated-Failure Review

When the user says `总是失败`, ask for recent concrete examples and diagnose the loop:

```text
最近三次你喜欢的人：
1. 怎么认识
2. 你为什么喜欢
3. 你做了什么
4. 对方怎么回应
5. 结束点在哪里
6. 你复盘出的原因
```

Look for one intervention at a time:

- more exposure
- better targeting
- profile rewrite
- simpler invitations
- slower pacing
- cleaner exit
- emotional regulation after rejection

## Safety Escalation

If the user expresses intent to stalk, threaten, self-harm, harm others, blackmail, or retaliate, stop dating coaching and focus on immediate safety, support people, emergency services, and de-escalation.
