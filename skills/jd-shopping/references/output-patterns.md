# Output Patterns

## Recommendation

State whether JD is a strong fit for this purchase.

## Product Comparison

Use a compact table when comparing options:

| Option | Visible Price | Seller Type | Trust Signal | Concern | Best For |
|--------|---------------|-------------|--------------|---------|----------|
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

## Why

Explain the main public signals: seller type, visible price, reviews, delivery hint, promotion conditions, and after-sales expectations.

## Caveats

Mention uncertain final price, account-only coupon eligibility, stock, address-dependent delivery, invoice/address checks, and seller policy differences.

## Cart-Ready Summary

When the user asks for shopping help, summarize the prepared cart state:

- Product and SKU
- Seller and trust reason
- Quantity
- Visible cart price or subtotal
- Visible coupon or promo notes
- Review caveats
- User-only next steps: login if needed, checkout settlement, address/invoice review, final confirmation, and payment

Never call the cart state an order preview or payment-ready state. Say the cart is prepared and the user controls settlement and payment.
